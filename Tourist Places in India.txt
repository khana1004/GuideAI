Tourist Places in India

1. Taj Mahal, Agra
   - A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. It is a symbol of love built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

2. Jaipur, Rajasthan
   - Known as the Pink City, Jaipur is famous for its palaces, forts, and vibrant culture. Major attractions include the Hawa Mahal, Amber Fort, and City Palace.

3. Kerala Backwaters
   - A network of interconnected canals, rivers, and lakes, offering scenic views and houseboat cruises. Popular destinations include Alleppey, Kumarakom, and Vembanad Lake.

4. Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh
   - One of the oldest cities in the world, known for its ghats along the Ganges River, spiritual significance, and vibrant culture.

5. Goa
   - Famous for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese-influenced architecture. Popular beaches include Baga, Anjuna, and Palolem.

6. Ladakh, Jammu & Kashmir
   - Known for its stunning landscapes, high-altitude desert, and Buddhist monasteries. Key attractions include Pangong Lake, Nubra Valley, and Leh Palace.

7. Mysore, Karnataka
   - Renowned for its royal heritage and the grand Mysore Palace. The city is also known for the annual Dussehra festival.

8. Rishikesh, Uttarakhand
   - A hub for yoga and adventure sports, located in the foothills of the Himalayas. Popular for white-water rafting, yoga retreats, and the Ganga Aarti.

9. Hampi, Karnataka
   - A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient temples and ruins from the Vijayanagara Empire. Notable sites include the Virupaksha Temple and Vittala Temple.

10. Andaman and Nicobar Islands
    - A group of islands known for their pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and diverse marine life. Popular spots include Havelock Island, Radhanagar Beach, and Cellular Jail.

11. Udaipur, Rajasthan
    - Known as the City of Lakes, famous for its stunning palaces and serene lakes. Major attractions include Lake Pichola, City Palace, and Jag Mandir.

12. Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh
    - A UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its intricate erotic sculptures and temples built between 950 and 1050 AD.

13. Sikkim
    - Known for its stunning landscapes, Buddhist monasteries, and the majestic Kanchenjunga mountain. Popular places include Gangtok, Pelling, and Tsomgo Lake.

14. Jaisalmer, Rajasthan
    - Known as the Golden City, famous for its sand dunes, Jaisalmer Fort, and camel safaris in the Thar Desert.

15. Darjeeling, West Bengal
    - Renowned for its tea gardens, panoramic views of the Himalayas, and the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

16. Amritsar, Punjab
    - Home to the Golden Temple, the holiest shrine of Sikhism. The city is also known for the Wagah Border ceremony and Jallianwala Bagh.

17. Rann of Kutch, Gujarat
    - A unique white salt desert, known for the Rann Utsav, a cultural festival held annually from November to February.

18. Sundarbans, West Bengal
    - A UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest mangrove forest in the world, home to the Royal Bengal Tiger.

19. Konark Sun Temple, Odisha
    - A 13th-century temple dedicated to the Sun God, renowned for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings.

20. Meenakshi Temple, Madurai, Tamil Nadu
    - A historic Hindu temple known for its stunning architecture, colorful gopurams, and rich cultural heritage.

21. Manali, Himachal Pradesh
    - A popular hill station known for its scenic beauty, adventure sports, and the Rohtang Pass.

22. Shimla, Himachal Pradesh
    - The capital of Himachal Pradesh, known for its colonial architecture, pleasant climate, and the Mall Road.

23. Agra Fort, Uttar Pradesh
    - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historic fort served as the residence of the Mughal emperors.

24. Fatehpur Sikri, Uttar Pradesh
    - A historic city and UNESCO World Heritage Site, once the capital of the Mughal Empire.

25. Ajanta and Ellora Caves, Maharashtra
    - UNESCO World Heritage Sites, known for their ancient rock-cut caves, Buddhist, Jain, and Hindu temples.

26. Mahabodhi Temple, Bodh Gaya, Bihar
    - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the temple where Buddha attained enlightenment.

30. Ellora Caves, Maharashtra

31. Kaziranga National Park, Assam

34. Munnar, Kerala

35. Pondicherry

36. Pushkar, Rajasthan

52. Gangotri, Uttarakhand
